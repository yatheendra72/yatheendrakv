There's a version of this post that starts with a grand declaration — a manifesto about writing in public, the value of building in the open, the importance of finding your voice. You've read it before. I won't write it.

Here's the honest version: I bought a domain, and now I have to put something on it.

But beneath the self-deprecation, there is something real. I think better when I write. Most of my ideas live as half-formed thoughts that feel profound at 11pm and dissolve by morning. Writing is the process of finding out which ones were actually worth keeping.

So this is where I'll do that.

## What you'll find here

No single topic. I'm an engineer who reads philosophy, watches too much sport, thinks about God more than is probably comfortable for people around me, and has strong opinions about films. All of that is fair game.

Some posts will be long. Some will be short. Some will be half-finished ideas I needed to get out of my head. I'm not optimizing for any particular audience — including, largely, SEO.

## What this isn't

A newsletter. A personal brand. A content strategy. I'm not building a funnel.

Just a place to think out loud.

If something here is useful or interesting to you, that's a happy accident. You're welcome.
